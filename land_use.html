Land Use - London, Ontario

Day 5: Land Use

London Ontario has a well-developed infrastructure having efficient transportation systems, diverse housing options, thriving commercial districts, industrial areas, and many public spaces. The city provides a rich variety of recreational facilities, from parks and sports centers to opportunities for outdoor activities along the Thames River, catering to diverse interests and promoting a vibrant community life.

Land Use Details:

  • Transportation (20%): Relies primarily on road networks, including major highways, and efficient public transit systems like buses.
    • Road Network: Well-connected by major highways and roads facilitating easy travel.
    • Public Transit: Efficient bus services offering convenient transportation within the city.
    • Wellington Road serves as a primary example of transportation within London Ontario
  • Housing (40%): Offers diverse options from residential neighborhoods to rural outskirts.
    • Residential Areas: Diverse housing options, including single-family homes, apartments, and townhouses.
    • Suburban Neighborhoods: Offers suburban living with ample amenities and proximity to the city.
    • Wortley Village is one of the oldest villages in london ontario since 1890 it currently houses around 16,000 people
  • Commercial (10%): Hosts various commercial districts and business hubs catering to diverse needs.
    • Shopping Centers: Various shopping malls and retail outlets catering to different preferences.
    • Business Hubs: Hosts commercial areas supporting diverse industries and businesses.
    • If you're a shopaholic, I strongly recoment visiting Richmond Row. It is home to many buisnesses and attracts many tourists.
  • Manufacturing (10%): Features industrial zones supporting manufacturing and production sectors.
    • London Ontario is filled with countless entrepeneurs and buisnesses in this image we can see one of the many industrial factories in London
  • Public Spaces (20%): Abundance of parks, recreational areas, and cultural spaces enhancing community engagement.
    • Parks & Recreational Areas: Abundance of parks, green spaces, and recreational facilities promoting community engagement.
    • Cultural Spaces: Museums, galleries, and cultural centers fostering art and heritage appreciation.
    • One of the most beautiful parks in London would be Victoria park. It has fantastic landscape and greenery plus amentities like playgrounds.
