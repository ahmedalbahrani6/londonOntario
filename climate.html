Climate - London, Ontario

Day 2: Climate

London has a continental climate. The average annual temperature is around 7-9°C (45-48°F). The total annual precipitation goes to around 1000 mm. The annual temperature range can vary significantly, usually going from sub-zero temperatures in winter to highs of around 30°C in summer.

Climate factors influencing London:

  • Latitude: Becasue of its higher latitude, London experiences seasonal changes in daylight hours.
  • Ocean currents: While not directly coastal, the Great Lakes affect local weather patterns, having an influenece on temperature and precipitation.
  • Winds: In london they have very strong winds which impact temperature and moisture levels, affecting weather conditions throughout the year.
  • Elevation: Even though its not that high,the elevation influences its temperature and precipitation patterns.
  • Topography: Surrounding mountains are often changing microclimates within London.
  • Bodies of water: Because of how close London is to the great lakes, its precipitation is often affected. In fact, London is less than an hour drive to 2 of teh great lakes!
All of these factors collectively affect London's cliamte. This allows London to be characterized as a city with an incredible variety in its climate.
